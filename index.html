Brief Information

Where To Go ?

There are some fascinating temples and Buddhist monasteries in the southern part of the Kathmandu Valley, but it’s hard to see too many together in a single day trip, as the villages are strung out on four different roads branching south from the Kathmandu Ring Rd. There’s a useful dirt-road shortcut that links the roads to Godavari and Chapagaon, and a walking-only route linking the road to Bungamati and Chobar on the way to Dakshinkali.

Where To Stay ?

At first, the chaos and traffic of Kathmandu can seem overwhelming, but behind the Third World metropolis is a hidden web of quiet courtyards and backstreets. The most serene and comfortable place to stay is 1 Dwarika’s Hotel. Designed along the lines of a traditional Buddhist courtyard, it includes original architectural features salvaged during Kathmandu’s building spree in the 1970s and ’80s. The inner courtyard contains trees and shrines and a pagoda

Hiking Tips

The most important thing to note while hiking in Nepal is altitude sickness. Many cases of death have been reported due to altitude sickness. If you fly to higher altitude. Stay there for two or three days and get used to altitude before you start your hike.

  • Bring layers of clothing
  • Pack sunscreen and bug spray
  • Carry extra water just in case
  • Pack light
